inshoreIQ Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

inshoreIQ helps saltwater anglers score fishing spots and log catches. This policy explains exactly what the app stores, what it sends where, and what is never shared. Your spots are the whole point, so we are specific about them.

The short version. Your spots and catches are yours. They are never sold, never pooled, and never shared with another user unless you deliberately send that person an invite. There are no ads, no analytics SDKs, and no cross-app tracking.

Using the app without an account

You can use inshoreIQ without signing in. In that mode your data is tied to a random sync code (for example POC-AB12C), not to your name, email, or device. Anyone who enters that same code can read that log. That is how sync between your own devices works, and it means the code is a shared secret you should treat like a password.

Signing in (optional)

If you choose Sign in with Apple, we receive:

  • A user ID from Apple, used to mark your spots and catches as yours.
  • An email address. If you choose "Hide My Email", Apple gives us a private relay address instead of your real one, and that is all we ever see.

We do not receive your name, contacts, or anything else from your Apple account.

Signing in lets you claim your data. Once claimed, the database itself enforces that only your account can read those rows. Not a setting we could flip: a rule the database applies to every request. No other user of inshoreIQ can read your claimed spots.

Sharing with a buddy

If you want someone else to see your log, you create an invite and send it to them. They can then read the log and add their own catches to it. You can revoke the invite at any time, and their access ends immediately. Nothing is shared unless you take that action.

What the app stores

  • Spots you create: name, coordinates, structure type, target species, your notes.
  • Catch log: species, count, length, bait, whether you kept or released it, your notes, and a snapshot of the conditions (tide, wind, pressure, water temperature, moon) at that time.
  • Settings: your sync code, home state, and on-water hours.
  • Account: the Apple user ID and email described above, only if you sign in.

This is stored on our backend, which runs on Supabase.

Location

Location is used only while you are using the app, to score spots near you, center the map, and set coordinates for spots you save. You can decline it and the app falls back to a default region. Your location is not tracked in the background and is not sold or shared.

Photos

If you photograph a fish or a lure to identify it, that single image is sent to our backend and on to an AI vision model to name the species or the lure. Photos are used for that request and are not retained by us, not attached to your account, and not used to train anything. The app never reads your photo library on its own: you choose the image, or you take it in the moment.

The AI features

The trip planner and pattern finder send your own log (spots, catches, conditions) to an AI model so it can answer questions about your fishing. This happens only when you tap one of those buttons. The data is used to produce your answer and is not used to train models or shared with other users.

Third-party data providers

To produce forecasts and scores the app sends coordinates only, never your identity, to public data services: NOAA (tides, water level, water temperature), Open-Meteo (weather, marine, sea surface temperature), USGS (river flow), and Esri, NOAA and state agency map services (bathymetry, habitat, navigation channels). None of these receive your name, email, account, or catch log.

What we do not do

  • We do not sell your data, to anyone, ever.
  • We do not pool your spot locations into any shared or "community" map.
  • We do not use advertising identifiers, ad networks, or cross-app tracking.
  • We do not run third-party analytics or crash-tracking SDKs.

If a future version ever offers an opt-in community feature, it would share only anonymized, location-stripped condition-to-outcome records (for example: falling tide with a north wind produced fish), with no coordinates, spot names, or identity, and only if you switch it on.

Deleting your data

  • Individual spots and catches: delete them in the app at any time.
  • Your whole account: Settings, then Account, then Delete account. This permanently deletes your account and every spot and catch you own. It cannot be undone.
  • No account: contact us with your sync code and we will delete every row under it.

Children

inshoreIQ is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect their data.
